Panchhi

Synopsis

Panchhi is set in the late 80s-early 90s.This tone is set by recognisable markers – the signature Akashvani tune and strains of Mile sur mera tumhara. The cast of four brings to life a middle class man’s struggle with hosting a simple evening of entertainment. Specifically, a celebratory meal with friends who treat him well... and often. The play follows this one evening – his fifth wedding anniversary – where he wants to spend the money he doesn’t have. So how does he keep creditors away and throw a party? The evening is peppered with domestic humour, and the all-too-familiar back-and-forths between a husband and wife. It also touches suggestively on economic deprivation and the middle-class aspirational. The relatability factor makes Panchhi’s engaging and bittersweet, while entertaining all the way.

Playwright: Manoj Mitra, Translated in Hindi by Md. Nizam, Asst Direction: Ankita Jain, Directed by Srinivas Beesetty.

Achievements: 23 National awards in various categories like Best Play, Best Actor (Male & Female), Best Set Design, Best Script, Best Actor in comic role and Best Director.
