Our Story

For ages, it has been important for kings to have a clown in their court. The clown was called Vidushak in India. Vidushak was the only person allowed to make fun of the king. He would mock the king, abuse the king, imitate the king’s mannerisms and openly say things no one else dared to. His caustic words and barbs and jibes enabled the king to question his own decisions, challenge his own thoughts. For that he had to pay a price: he had to dress like a fool and look like an idiot.

Now coming to the part as to why I relate to this “not-so-smart” character so much so that I started seeing myself in this guy. I have never agreed to the masses, never followed the herd blindly, but at the same time I never really had the courage to rebel and shout out. I started doing what the Vidushak did, questioning everything under the façade of some other character. Living the reality in this imagination made life so much more beautiful. So, for the past decade, I have been the Vidushak... :)

To sum up, the villain does the wrong, the hero rights the wrong, the Vidushak questions the right!
